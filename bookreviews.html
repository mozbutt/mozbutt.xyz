📖 Book Reviews

Critical reflections on legal theory, systems thinking, computation, and adjacent texts.

📘 The Concept of Law – H.L.A. Hart

Summary: Hart reconstructs legal positivism using the distinction between primary and secondary rules, and offers a procedural account of legal authority.

Review: A foundational text, but its treatment of discretion lacks formal constraint. Strong on conceptual clarity, weak on auditability under complex systems.

📗 The Inner Morality of Law – Lon Fuller

Summary: Fuller argues that law contains an internal morality: consistency, publicity, prospectivity, and congruence between rules and enforcement.

Review: Closer to a procedural ethic than a moral code. Useful in evaluating system legitimacy, but assumes too much about interpretive convergence.
